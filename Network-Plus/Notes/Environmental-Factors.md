# Data Center Environmental Factors

## 1. Energy Consumption

Data centers are massive energy consumers, operating 24/7.

* **Impact**: Approximately **2% of all US power consumption** is attributed to data centers.
* **Usage**: A significant portion of this power is dedicated to environmental controls rather than just the computing equipment.

---

## 2. Humidity Management

Maintaining precise humidity levels is critical for equipment longevity and safety.

* **Ideal Range**: **40% to 60%** humidity.
* **High Humidity Risks**: Leads to **condensation**, which can cause short circuits or corrosion in electrical components.
* **Low Humidity Risks**: Leads to excessive **static discharge**, which can damage sensitive electronic components.

---

## 3. Temperature Control

HVAC systems are engineered to keep equipment within an optimal operating range.

* **Target Range**: **64°F to 81°F** (17.7°C to 27°C).
* **Load Factors**: Cooling needs fluctuate based on outdoor temperatures and the internal "system load" (heat generated by servers under heavy use).
* **Consistency**: Sensors are placed throughout the facility to ensure uniform temperature and humidity across the entire floor.

---

## 4. Fire Suppression

Standard water-based sprinklers are often avoided in data centers due to the high density of electrical equipment.

* **Chemical/Gas Agents**: Specialized tanks contain **inert gases** or chemical agents designed to suppress fire or remove oxygen from the room.
* **HVAC Integration**: When a fire is detected, the HVAC system **automatically shuts down** to prevent fresh oxygen from feeding the flames.
* **Containment**: While these chemicals may damage hardware, they are vital for preventing the fire from spreading to the rest of the facility.
